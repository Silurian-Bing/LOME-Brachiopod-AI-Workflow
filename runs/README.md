# Workflow Runs

This folder contains public-safe examples of intermediate workflow runs. The records are reconstructed from internal diagnostic notes, figure-redesign notes, review comments, and execution logs. They are intended to show how human review, agent coordination, and skills-based computation can be documented in an auditable paleobiological workflow.

The records deliberately omit unpublished core interpretations, final curated datasets, final publication figures, and sensitive research framing. They should be read as workflow-demonstration traces rather than complete reproducibility packages.

## Included Runs

- `run_001_standardization_correction`: correction of the spatial-standardization layer after a mismatch with the established mapping framework.
- `run_002_figure_redesign`: human-guided redesign of intermediate figures after visual and narrative redundancy was identified.
- `run_003_sampling_spatial_diagnostics`: diagnostic run for sampling structure, coordinate consistency, and equal-area spatial summaries.
- `run_004_statistical_review_boundary`: independent review of statistical interpretation boundaries and required robustness checks.
- `run_005_pyrate_reliability_review`: review of PyRate settings, effective sample size, and interpretation limits.

## Public-Safety Rules

These run records avoid:

- unpublished core claims;
- final expert-curated data;
- final figure panels;
- sensitive internal figure names or narrative labels;
- local absolute paths.

They preserve:

- the workflow problem;
- the reason human review intervened;
- the agent/skill modules involved;
- the decision made after review;
- the public-safe outputs or follow-up tasks.
