# Knowledge Card 06: Uncertainties and Frontiers

## Scope

This card records disputes and unresolved points that should be retrieved whenever the system is asked to produce strong conclusions.

## Key Uncertainties

The number of extinction pulses remains debated. The classic two-pulse model is still a necessary baseline, but extended or multi-pulse interpretations have gained attention in recent work. These alternatives should be treated as competing temporal models rather than as simple replacements.

The dominant kill mechanism remains disputed. Cooling, glaciation, warming, volcanism, ocean redox change, sea-level change, and ecological selectivity are all part of the literature. A robust answer should describe the scale and proxy behind each claim.

Post-extinction cosmopolitanism is difficult to interpret. It may reflect true biogeographic mixing, low diversity, survival of broad-ranging taxa, loss of endemic taxa, or sampling effects.

Similarity and network metrics are not neutral. Results can change with taxonomic rank, region definition, temporal binning, low-sampling cells, and similarity coefficient.

Recent or future-year references require manual verification before manuscript citation. The RAG folder therefore keeps a `needs_verification.csv` file that should not be indexed as factual evidence.
