# Knowledge Card 02: Brachiopod Paleobiogeography Around LOME

## Scope

This card summarizes the brachiopod literature most relevant to a LOME paleobiogeographic workflow.

## Core Claims

The Hirnantia fauna is the central brachiopod reference system for the crisis interval. Classic work divided latest Ordovician brachiopod faunas into broad paleobiogeographic patterns that are usually discussed in relation to temperature, substrate, water depth, and glacial sea-level change (`rong_harper_1988_hirnantian`; `rong_chen_harper_2002_hirnantia`; `rong_etal_2020_hirnantian_insights`).

South China is not merely a point on a global map. It preserves ecologically differentiated Hirnantian brachiopod assemblages and therefore supports a finer question: whether apparent global cosmopolitanism hides local or regional heterogeneity (`huang_rong_2024_heterogeneous`).

Global brachiopod paleobiogeographic change can be analyzed together with diversity dynamics. The PBDB-based work by Shi and Huang provides a direct precedent for linking diversity fluctuations, endemic/cosmopolitan components, and paleobiogeographic distribution shifts across the extinction and recovery interval (`shi_huang_2024_synchronicity`).

Post-LOME brachiopod recovery should be treated as staged and taxonomically uneven. Early Silurian diversification involves both surviving lineages and new taxonomic/geographic patterns, so a workflow must distinguish survival, immigration, general faunal affinity, and genuine local continuity (`huang_chen_shi_2025_post_lome`).

Deep-water and warm-water recovery faunas complicate a simple latitude-only model. Edgewood-Cathay-related material and its Hirnantian counterparts show that facies, bathymetry, and regional pathways need to be kept explicit in the evidence chain (`baarli_huang_johnson_2024_edgewood_cathay`).

## Use in Manuscript

Use this card for the case-study chapter. It justifies a workflow that starts from a human-defined paleobiogeographic question and then combines RAG, PBDB/GBDB access, taxonomic cleaning, stratigraphic binning, paleogeographic reconstruction, diversity analysis, and review.

## Review Gate

Do not collapse different faunal concepts into one label. Hirnantia, Edgewood, Edgewood-Cathay, Cathaysiorthis, and post-LOME recovery faunas may differ in age, facies, geography, and taxonomic composition.
