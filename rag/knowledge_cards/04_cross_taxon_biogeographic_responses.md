# Knowledge Card 04: Cross-Taxon Biogeographic Responses

## Scope

This card summarizes cross-taxon patterns around LOME for comparison with brachiopod behavior.

## Core Claims

Different clades responded to LOME at different spatial and temporal scales. Brachiopods, graptolites, corals, trilobites, cephalopods, acritarchs, and chitinozoans do not form one synchronized biological signal.

Graptolites show a sharp turnover from older diplograptine-dominated faunas toward normalograptid and neograptine dominance. This makes graptolites powerful for biostratigraphy and extinction timing, but their planktonic ecology should not be equated with benthic brachiopod response.

Acritarch and chitinozoan records emphasize changes in phytoplankton provincialism and cosmopolitanism. These patterns are valuable for testing whether apparent benthic homogenization has a broader planktonic counterpart, but facies and preservation must be treated carefully.

Corals and reef systems generally recovered more slowly than many brachiopod assemblages. Coral data are useful for detecting warm-water recovery and carbonate platform restructuring, not simply for measuring the same pattern as brachiopods.

Trilobite and cephalopod records highlight selective extinction, habitat loss, and the role of refugia. These records help frame the limits of any brachiopod-only inference.

## Review Gate

Do not use evidence from planktonic groups to directly infer benthic dispersal without stating the ecological difference.
