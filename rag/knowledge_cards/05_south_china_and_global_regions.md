# Knowledge Card 05: South China and Global Regional Context

## Scope

This card summarizes why South China and selected global regions matter for LOME paleobiogeography.

## Core Claims

South China is a core region for LOME research because it combines biostratigraphy, chemostratigraphy, high-precision dating, fossil assemblages, and environmental records. It should be treated as a high-resolution local laboratory rather than merely as one geographic node.

The Yangtze Platform includes shallow and deeper-water records, making it important for testing whether faunal patterns reflect global crisis, local bathymetry, regional tectonics, or facies structure.

Other key regions include Baltica/Oslo, Anticosti Island, Laurentian midcontinent and margins, North Africa/Gondwana, Sibumasu, and Tarim. Each region contributes a different part of the evidence chain: standard sections, glacial deposits, recovery faunas, deep-water assemblages, or cross-plate affinities.

Global paleobiogeographic interpretation should avoid treating province counts as literal facts independent of sampling. Apparent cosmopolitanism after extinction can reflect real dispersal, reduced diversity, broader environmental tolerance, or a statistical inability to distinguish provinces when diversity is low.
